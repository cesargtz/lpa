Being the master of your own time in Paraguay

Being the master of your own time in Paraguay

May 07, 2015
By
Rebecca Rouse

When was the last time your bank let you know how much they valued your time? Better yet, when was the last time they proved it to you? For most of us, waiting in line is just a part of life. Applying for a loan? Get in line. Picking up a remittance? The line starts here. Right? Not necessarily.

Last week in the city of Luque, Paraguay, I had the chance to tour a new model of bank branch that is trying to reinvent the customer experience. That bank is Visión Banco, one of the beneficiaries of the MIF’s Remittances and Savings Program.  Visión Banco is rolling out updated branches all over the country that use physical space – and technology – to streamline transactions and make sure that clients get what they need, without the hassle.

Their new branches feature an anteroom that the client enters before they even get to the lobby, containing interactive kiosks, telephones that connect them directly to customer service representatives, and ATMs which allow them to manage their own transactions and move through processes more quickly. A self-service kiosk lets customers fill out loan applications immediately before they even get in line, passing the data directly to a loan officer so that by the time they talk to a real person, all the necessary forms are pre-populated with data and awaiting a signature, and a lending decision has been made. Simpler transactions such as deposits, withdrawals, and balance inquiries can be done directly on the ATMs without the need for a human cashier. In the anteroom the clients can also alert the bank to the fact that they are there to pick up a remittance to receive expedited service. The best part of this model is the fact that customer service representatives are available in the anterooms to help customers who are unsure about using technology on their own at first, teaching them how to operate the ATMs and the kiosk, and answering questions.

Is it successful? The proof is in the patterns of use: the anterooms are open to customers on the weekends and evenings when the bank is closed, and the number of users during after hours, that is, during times when the customer service representatives are not present, continues to grow exponentially. Visión Banco believes that this is a sign that customers, once they are taught how to use the ATMs, are now more comfortable using them on their own at times that are convenient for them, taking control of their own transactions. Waiting in line cannot be completely avoided, of course, and Visión Banco has also invested a lot in making sure that their waiting rooms are spacious and comfortable, even offering coloring books and crayons to customers who arrive with children.

Streamlining these processes is great for the bank’s image, and certainly goes a long way towards client retention. But there is also a more immediate business case for it: one of Visión Banco’s biggest ongoing challenges has been managing foot traffic in their branches, dealing with scores of customers waiting to speak to a cashier for simple requests such as checking an account balance. Even though the bank has long offered ATMs, many customers were not yet comfortable using the machines and preferred in-person transactions, thus putting a strain on branch staff and preventing them from serving more people effectively.

These types of innovations also make Visión Banco more competitive in the remittance market. In country where remittance companies pay through a variety of financial institutions, customers have many options when it comes to how they choose to send and receive their remittance. Today, the bank is working with the MIF to expand the use of simplified accounts that allow customers to receive money via direct deposit, and then access it at any ATM at the moment most convenient for them.

As the new Remittances and Savings Program project takes off, the bank’s customer-centric approach is sure to be a critical component its success. This new service model could be just the thing that differentiates Visión Banco from the rest of the pack.

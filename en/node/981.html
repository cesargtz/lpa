The MIF and the Coca Cola Company announce US$16 million social investment in Latin America

The MIF and the Coca Cola Company announce US$16 million social investment in Latin America

December 09, 2013
By
Georg Neumann
  • The IDB’s Multilateral Investment Fund and the Coca-Cola Brazil Institute will strengthen the developmental impact ofthe Coletivo model and implement pilot projects in the 2014 FIFA World Cup™ host cities;
  • Partnership will accelerate the expansion of the program, which is part of Coca-Cola’s global 5by20 initiative focused on entrepreneurial women.

Rio de Janeiro, December 09, 2013 – The Coca-Cola Company (TCCC) and the Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the IDB Group, announced today, during the Clinton Global Initiative Latin America, an investment of US$ 16 million to expand the Coca-Cola Coletivo platform.  This expansion will build on Coletivo’s strong presence in Brazil and also implement pilot projects in three Latin American countries.

Coletivo (collective in English) promotes economic opportunities for entrepreneurial women and youth in low-income communities, by working with local institutions to provide job and entrepreneurial training, life skills and access to markets. The expansion announced today is expected to benefit an initial 30,000 people in Brazil alone.

“As we expand Coletivo’s reach and scale, it’s inspiring to know we will help thousands of people to transform their own lives through our core business,” says Xiemar Zarazúa, President of Coca-Cola Brasil.  “We firmly believe in the power of ‘the Golden Triangle’ of business, government and civil society working together to bring real solutions to critical global challenges.“

A team from the MIF and the Coca-Cola Brazil Institute will work together to expand job training for youth, help women grow their own businesses and strengthen recycling cooperatives in Brazil and three other Latin American countries. The new model will be piloted in the 12 host cities of the 2014 FIFA World Cup™ (Manaus, Fortaleza, Natal, Recife, Salvador, Cuiabá, Brasília, Belo Horizonte, São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Curitiba and Porto Alegre).

Nancy Lee, MIF´s General Manager says “Coletivo is a shared value business model that uses part of the value generated to benefit poor communities, particularly youth and women. We believe that this model can be a powerful and growing force for empowering excluded populations. The MIF will deploy its experience around the Region to help scale this model and make it more sustainable.”

In 2009, Coca-Cola Brazil launched Coletivo, aimed to help women and youth increase incomes and self-confidence. As a result of the partnership announced today, the enhanced Coletivo model will be applied in three programs: Retail, Entrepreneurship and Recycling.  Coletivo Retail focuses on young people in urban low-income communities who are seeking formal employment.  Coletivo Recycling helps to professionalize recycling cooperatives, generating increased income for waste collectors and formalizing the recycling value chain.  Coletivo Entrepreneurship provides practical training to begin and strengthen new businesses, with a focus on women.  In addition to technical training, all Coletivo programs leverage the Company's value chain to connect participants with new markets and incorporate life skills training.  This proven methodology increases self-confidence and encourages individuals to shape their own future. 

To date, Coletivo has 500 programs in 150 Brazilian communities, impacting more than 70,000 people.  The initiative is a Shared Value program that aims to benefit society through the Company's value chain.  

The expansion of the Coletivo platform is a decisive step for Coca-Cola as part of its ambitious global 5by20 initiative, which intends to enable the economic empowerment of five million women across the Company's value chain by 2020.  During the past two years, the 5by20 initiative has impacted 300,000 women around the world.  Brazil is one of the four leading countries to adopt the initiative (alongside India, South Africa and the Philippines), and is considered a reference for economic empowerment in Latin America.

About Coca-Cola Brazil

The Coca-Cola Brazil System operates in eight segments of the non-alcoholic beverage sector - waters, teas, soft drinks, nectars/refreshments, energy drinks, isotonic drinks and dairy drinks, with a line of more than 125 products that include regular flavors and low-calorie versions.  Formed by Coca-Cola Brazil and 12 Brazilian bottling groups, the System employs 66 thousand people directly and generates about 600 thousand indirect jobs.  The investments of the Coca-Cola Brazil System in 2013 will amount to R$ 2,6 billion.  Between 2012 and 2016, a total of R$ 14,1 billion will be invested, 50% more than between 2007 and 2011.  Sustainability is a commitment of Coca-Cola Brazil, which is reflected in the way the company and its bottlers address people and the environment.  For example,     Coca-Cola Brazil's water use rate is one of the best in the world:  1,91 liters of water per liter of beverage produced - less than half the volume used 13 years ago.  In recycling Coca-Cola Brazil developed, through the Coca-Cola Brazil Institute, the program Coletivo Recycling, formerly known as "Recycle and Win" which, since 1996 has helped to turn the Country into one of the most efficient in recycling of materials.  Today, 98% of the aluminum cans and 56% of the PET packages are recycled.  To learn more, please visit the websites www.institutococacolabrasil.com.br and www.cocacolabrasil.com.br.

About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the IDB Group, is funded by 39 donors and supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models.

MIF to support community-based waste management system in Guatemala’s Lake Atitlan Watershed

MIF to support community-based waste management system in Guatemala’s Lake Atitlan Watershed

February 27, 2014
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, has approved a US$ 1,146,220 technical cooperation grant to support SOCODEVI (the Canadian Cooperation Society for International Development)  in providing better access to water and sanitation services and solid waste treatment in Guatemala’s Lake Atitlan watershed. The project, which will be carried out in four municipalities around the lake with a total population of more than 160,000 people, will reduce the amount of wastewater and trash flowing into the basin.  In order to achieve this, SOCODEVI will pilot an integrated and sustainable system for operations and maintenance of wastewater plants through municipally supervised community waste management entities. The Lake Atitlan project also aims to increase family incomes through the creation of new jobs related to waste management and to selling recycled materials and organic compost.

Currently, 95% of domestic wastewater is untreated and more than 7 million cubic meters of untreated wastewater flows into Lake Atitlan annually. The watershed’s predominantly Mayan indigenous population is especially vulnerable to health issues related to untreated waste. While there has been significant investment in infrastructure for sewage and garbage management, as well as a number of local initiatives to provide waste services in the Lake Atitlan watershed area, there is no entity technically responsible for the sustainable management of operations and maintenance, and there is little coordination among the different stakeholders in the local market for recyclables and compost. Additionally, there are low levels of environmental awareness in the region.

This project will first help establish waste management entities under the supervision of the municipalities, which will hold responsibility for operations and maintenance, and will implement tariff systems for long-term sustainability. Simultaneously, the project will raise the communities’ environmental awareness, and will create alliances to enhance and expand the local market for recycled materials and compost.

This initiative is complementary to an IDB loan and technical assistance project (GU-L1039) for upgrading and expanding water and sanitation services and management in Guatemala. The MIF operation will extend this community engagement to three additional municipalities in Atitlan and will provide technical assistance for the operation and maintenance of existing water and waste treatment facilities. In the long term the project expects to be transformative for the Lake Atitlan watershed, as it will create a model that can be replicated by the remaining 11 communities surrounding the lake, considerably reducing contamination and risk to the livelihoods of the people living around the lake, and increasing tourism-related activities.

 

About the MIF
 

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, is funded by 39 donors and supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models. More information at www.fomin.org
 

About SOCODEVI
 

SOCODEVI (Canadian Cooperation Society for International Development) is a Canadian NGO that supports vulnerable populations by focusing on collective and cooperative approaches that promote autonomy and capacity building in order to reduce poverty. It has worked with 700 cooperative and mutual enterprises and organizations in 40 developing countries.

FOROMIC 2016, Latin America and the Caribbean’s largest entrepreneurship conference to be held for the first time in Jamaica

FOROMIC 2016, Latin America and the Caribbean’s largest entrepreneurship conference to be held for the first time in Jamaica

July 18, 2016
By
Claudia Moreno

FOROMIC to provide MSMEs increased access to low-cost financing for productive projects – Minister of Finance, Audley Shaw 

Latin America and the Caribbean’s largest and most important annual event on financial inclusion, financial innovation and entrepreneurship will be held in Jamaica from October 24 to 26 at the Montego Bay Convention Centre. This is the first time that Jamaica will be hosting FOROMIC, an international event that gathers around 1,500 participants from over 40 countries annually to share knowledge and experiences on entrepreneurship. Spearheaded and organized by the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) through the Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), FOROMIC 2016 will focus on the topic: “Creating Opportunities through Financial Inclusion.”

FOROMIC is one of the main platforms in the region to discuss topics related to the development and promotion of Micro, Small and Medium-Sized Enterprises (MSMEs), financial inclusion and entrepreneurship. Its objective is to promote the dissemination of knowledge, to create new synergies among the various actors and to foster innovation.

“We are very excited about the focus of this year’s conference. The IDB is very big on advancing high impact entrepreneurship among MSMEs and facilitating access to key financial support and services is integral to achieving that important development objective,” noted Therese Turner-Jones, General Manager for the Inter-American Development Bank’s Caribbean Country Department. “When we support MSMEs in this way, we are in fact improving lives, which is the core objective of the IDB in all the activities and projects that we pursue.”

A key partner for the staging of FOROMIC 2016, the Government of Jamaica through the Ministry of Finance and the Public Service is working with the IDB to ensure that local entrepreneurs benefit from the conference.

"MSMEs account for approximately 90% of jobs in the Jamaican economy and as such are critical stakeholders to the economic growth and development programme. MSMEs facilitate wealth creation, generate employment, and are pivotal to the expansion of the private sector and in facilitating a private sector lead engine of growth. The Government of Jamaica is committed to the development of this sector and has taken bold steps with a multiplicity of strategies that will lead to sustainability of the MSME partnership. FOROMIC is expected to provide our MSMEs with a plethora of opportunities for increased access to low cost financing for productive projects and for networking which will aid in the unleashing of the well needed entrepreneurial dynamism within our economy. I invite all our entrepreneurial partners to join us at FOROMIC Jamaica 2016, and together let us exploit the opportunities presented as we aggressively pursue a partnership to prosperity,” said the Hon. Audley Shaw, CD, MP, Minister of Finance and the Public Service.

MSMEs in Jamaica and across the region are urged to register early for FOROMIC 2016 in order to secure their opportunity to benefit from the conference. MSMEs can register by visiting www.foromic.org  “Now in its 19th year, FOROMIC has been a sought-after conference across the region given the magnitude of the network, knowledge-sharing and business opportunities to be garnered. With the support of the Government of Jamaica, our partners and sponsors, the IDB is very happy that MSMEs in Jamaica will be able to more feasibly access the benefits of FOROMIC in 2016,” expressed Turner-Jones.

The three-day conference will also include the recognition of the winners of the Inter-American Awards for Financial and Entrepreneurial Innovation. 

About the IDB

The Inter-American Development Bank is devoted to improving lives. Established in 1959, the IDB is a leading source of long-term financing for economic, social and institutional development in Latin America and the Caribbean. The IDB also conducts cutting-edge research and provides policy advice, technical assistance and training to public and private sector clients throughout the region. The IDB is the leading source of multilateral financing for Latin America and the Caribbean.

About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund is the innovation lab for the Inter-American Development Bank Group. It conducts high-risk experiments to test new models for engaging and inspiring the private sector to solve economic development problems in Latin America and the Caribbean. The MIF addresses poverty and vulnerability by focusing on emerging businesses and smallholder farmers with the capacity to grow and create economic opportunities. www.fomin.org

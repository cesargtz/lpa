What is Climate-Smart Agriculture?

Climate-Smart Agriculture includes models in agriculture, fisheries, livestock and forestry that employ technology, financing, and value chain structures that Â boost smallholder productivity, increase incomes, and build resilience along the value chain. Where did the concept came from?

CSA was borne out of a concern that fishery, livestock and agriculture technologies and practices were increasingly unsustainable: they had become a major contributor to the inefficient use of natural resources, carbon emissions, the degradation of soils, deforestation, and the destruction of coastal resources. In addition, innovation in the agriculture area is increasingly important as the world seeks to feed more people using fewer resources.

Closing the information gap on social impact bonds

Closing the information gap on social impact bonds

September 02, 2015
By
Zachary Levey

Are Latin American governments interested in paying only for what works? Are there investors who want to link their financial returns to improved social outcomes? Can governments, private investors, and nonprofits work together to solve intractable social problems? These are some of the questions we asked ourselves at the Inter-American Development Bank’s Multilateral Investment Fund, before launching a regional program to support social impact bonds (SIBs) and results-based financing models in Latin America and the Caribbean.

A SIB is a partnership in which impact investors (investors that seek social impact as well as financial returns) and philanthropic organizations—not governments—take on the risk of financing a solution to a difficult social problem. Our goal was to launch the first SIBs in Latin America and mobilize local ecosystems that could support investment in social impact. In a little over one year, SIBs are rapidly taking hold in Latin America. Several governments are designing the region’s first such partnerships, and a growing number of foundations, nongovernmental organizations, universities, and investors have become active proponents of the model.

However, major challenges remain. When we began engaging potentially interested parties in Latin America, we quickly realized that very few of them had ever heard of SIBs. Despite global attention on the model, language was a barrier and there was very little content and information in Spanish and Portuguese.

As part of our effort to close the information gap, we are excited to release this animated video that provides an introduction to SIBs that can be shared and understood by a diverse audience. Please watch and share with your colleagues, bosses, clients, and partners.

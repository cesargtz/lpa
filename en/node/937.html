MIF and Finca Peru seek to prevent violence against women through an innovative microfinance program

January 15, 2014
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group has approved a US$765,590 technical cooperation grant for a project led by Finca Peru, which seeks, Â through microfinance, to prevent violence against more than 1,000 women living in suburban and rural communities in the Peruvian departments of Huancavelica and Ayacucho . To achieve this, the project will adapt the Intervention with Microfinance for AIDS and Gender Equity (IMAGE) model, a microcredit program implemented in South Africa that combines microfinance with education and participatory workshops in order to tackle gender norms and roles, enhance communication skills, improve womenâs influence in household decisions and create collective action mechanisms to combat violence against women.Â  In Latin America, violence against women is a serious problem and generates costs related to health care, social protection services and justice. In 2011, 28% of women aged 15- 49 in Peru reported being physically abused by a partner at some point in their life, and 12.6% reported having been abused in the past 12 months. Despite the magnitude and complexity of this problem, there is little scientific evidence of the effectiveness of violence against women prevention programs in Latin America. The IMAGE intervention is one of the few programs worldwidepresenting solid evidence. Microfinance institutions have great potential to address violence against women. First, economic empowerment generated through credit and other financial services is a basic tool for taking concrete action against violence. In addition, frequent access to institutions by women can contribute to the sustainability of training on these issues. This initiative constitutes a joint effort between the MIF and several other entities of the Inter-American Development Bank: the Citizen Security Initiative (, the Gender and Diversity Unit, the Institutional Capacity of State Division, and the Office of Strategic Planning and Development Effectiveness. About the MIF The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, is funded by 39 donors and supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models.

New Youth and Entrepreneurship Program announced for Latin America and the Caribbean

New Youth and Entrepreneurship Program announced for Latin America and the Caribbean

September 10, 2013
By
Claudia Moreno

 

September 10, 2013

Yesterday, the Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, and Youth Business International (YBI) launched the Youth Entrepreneurship Program (YEP), a joint initiative which will provide training and other supports to low-income youth seeking to start businesses in Latin America and the Caribbean. The announcement was made during the 2013 Global Youth Entrepreneurship Summit, hosted by YBI in London, England.

Over the next 5 years, YEP plans to provide targeted enterprise training to almost 65,000 poor and low-income people between the ages of 18 and 20 in up to 10 countries in Latin America and the Caribbean. An estimated 28,000 new businesses will be created and/or strengthened as a result of the training. YEP will engage with corporations, public sector agencies, nongovernmental organizations and others to develop the youth entrepreneurship ecosystems in the selected countries. As part of YEP, the MIF and YBI will also establish a Regional Center for Youth Entrepreneurship that will serve as a dedicated source of knowledge and support for all regional stakeholders working with young entrepreneurs.

 

The Latin American and Caribbean region has a high youth unemployment rate—13% of youth are unemployed, compared to 5% of adults, and at least 50 million of the poor and low-income young people in the region are considered “at risk.”

 

To address such issues, many young people are interested in starting their own businesses; but usually face multiple barriers in doing so, including a lack of education on business practices, limited access to financing, and complex regulatory environments for business start-ups. Additionally, there is scarce research-based evidence about how best to support disadvantaged youth in starting and growing businesses. The MIF and YBI will lead the design of training programs offered, based on their joint and separate experience in youth employment and entrepreneurship training, and will commission rigorous evaluations of training programs in order to identify lessons learned and other valuable information on making such programs more effective.

 

YEP is the latest in a series of joint projects between the MIF and YBI—the MIF has been a leading sponsor of YBI’s programs in Latin America and the Caribbean.

 

“There are 148 million people aged 15 to 29 years old in Latin America and the Caribbean, and one in five are neither studying nor in work,” said Claudio Cortellese, chief of the MIF’s Access to Markets and Skills Unit. “By helping young people secure the skills and capital that they need to start a business, the YBI network can support many thousands of young people in the region, and the MIF is proud to be supporting their work.”

 

“There are fundamental challenges for young people from disadvantaged socio-economic backgrounds in Latin America and the Caribbean who are interested in starting and growing a successful business,” said Andrew Devenport, Chief Executive of Youth Business International. “There is currently a lack of evidence about how best to address these challenges across the region, and a scarcity of tested and scalable support models. This program aims to redress this, combining the experience, resources and contacts of the MIF with those of the YBI network.”

 

About the MIF

 

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank Group, is funded by 39 donors and supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models. More information at www.fomin.org.

 

About Youth Business International

 

Founded in 2000 by HRH The Prince of Wales, Youth Business International (YBI) is a global network of independent non-profit initiatives helping young people start and grow their own business and create employment. YBI supports under-served young entrepreneurs, aged 18-35, with a combination of training, access to capital, mentoring, and other business development services. YBI members have helped over 100,000 young people to create their own business and generate employment. An estimated 70% of these businesses are still trading after three years, with many going on to create significant levels of employment and further benefits for their communities. More information at youthhbusiness.org

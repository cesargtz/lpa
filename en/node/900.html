MIF to support Health Microinsurance in Bolivia

MIF to support Health Microinsurance in Bolivia

March 13, 2014
By
María Paula Brito Rozas

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, has approved a $900,000 grant to support the Bolivian health care provider network  Programa de Coordinación en Salud Integral (PROCOSI, or Coordinated Comprehensive Health Program in English)  in providing microinsurance products that cover basic health plans.

The project will benefit a total of 60,000 low-income people, 60% of whom are expected to be women and children, in urban and periurban areas of La Paz and El Alto, Quillacollo, Cochabamba, Yapacani, Montero, and Santa Cruz de la Sierra.

In Bolivia, the supply of health services for low-income populations is limited. In 2010, only 31% of the population had access to health insurance, primarily because Bolivian social security covers wage earners and excludes families that earn their living through self-employment or agricultural activity.

In order to provide low-income households the financial means to deal with serious sickness or accidents, the project will design, test, and implement an insurance product that uses a prepaid card for basic health care coverage. Through this microinsurance business model, out-of-pocket health expenditures of low-income households can be reduced, which will allow beneficiaries to maintain their income in the event of a health emergency. 

The project will develop a partnership between PROCOSI, an insurance company, and a microfinance institution. The primary clients of this financial product will be clients of the microfinance institution. The prepaid card will be backed by PROCOSI’s health service providers and an insurance company will underwrite the risk of health emergencies covered by the policy. 

About the MIF 

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, is funded by 39 donors and supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models. More information at www.fomin.org 

About PROCOSI

PROCOSI (Programa de Coordinación en Salud Integral) is a nongovernmental organization (NGO) active in the nine departments of Bolivia. It is composed of 31 national and international member organizations, which makes it the largest network of comprehensive health and development NGOs in the country. PROCOSI has implemented 15 projects with national scope.

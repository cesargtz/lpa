Low-income youth will be given skills and guidance to break into the employment market in Peru

Low-income youth will be given skills and guidance to break into the employment market in Peru

December 16, 2015
By
María Paula Brito Rozas

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, has recently approved a US$1,523,684 technical cooperation grant for the project “New Employment Opportunities for Youth in Peru (NEO)” that will be executed by the Center for Employment Training and Development Services – CAPLAB. The project seeks to equip poor and vulnerable youth with the skills they need to be more competitive in the labor market, and offer improved employment services that will increase their chances of finding quality employment. The project will benefit more than 14,000 young people ages 16 to 29, of which at least 50% are women, through public-private partnership between business, government, and civil society organizations. The project is part of the program “New Employment Opportunities for Youth (NEO),” which was launched in 2012 and aims to improve the employability of one million poor and vulnerable youth in Latin America and the Caribbean by 2022. 

Throughout the last decade Peru’s economic growth has translated to improvements in employment indicators, however, this growth has not spread equally to all sectors of society, leaving low-incomeyouth behind as they struggle to find quality jobs. The youth unemployment rate (7.8%) is three times higher than for adults (2.1%), and more than 50% of working youth have informal and seasonal jobs. 

To address the troubles young people face when seeking employment, this project tackles the issues on various fronts: strengthening employability service providers by training their professionals to offer services tailored to the needs of youth, gearing training to focus on life skills and vocational guidance, and aligning counseling with labor market demands. Likewise the project aims to improve information sharing on job vacancies between employment offices and the Ministry of Labor and Employment Promotion, in order to facilitate an ongoing dialogue between public, private, and civil society providers of employment services, to offer more specialized services to vulnerable youth.

Another important component of the project is identifying labor market demand for youth by seeking systematic input from senior executives of private companies on positions they look to fill. By organizing business roundtables, the project aims to foster productive sector participation in the development of training programs.

In the longer term, the various components of this project seek to make an impact on the youth employability systems as a whole, by validating an effective employability model that can be replicated and transferred to other productive sectors and to the thousands of vocational and technical training institutions in the country. 

About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, is funded by 39 countries and supports private sector-led development benefiting low-income populations and the poor—their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory—experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks to build and support successful micro, small, and medium business models. Learn more at www.fomin.org

About CAPLAB

The Center for Employment Training and Development Services – CAPLAB is a civil society non-profit organization that manages development projects focused on strengthening human capital in coherence with the needs and rights of people, as well as the socioeconomic development of the country, through technical training and job training that is aligned with local, regional and national opportunities. CAPLAB is a specialized entity that executes development projects in Peru and several other countries in the region, providing research, consulting, training, technical assistance, advisory support, and management services. Learn more at http://www.caplab.org.pe/

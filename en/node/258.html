Building Capacity to Develop Sustainable PPPs: A New Global Certification

Building Capacity to Develop Sustainable PPPs: A New Global Certification

June 09, 2016
By
Guest

By Jyoti Bisbey and Leopoldo Montañez

Governments around the world now understand that public-private partnerships (PPPs) can be an important option to help meet infrastructure and basic service needs while contributing to a reduction in poverty. By bundling construction and service delivery, PPPs offer the possibility of improved accountability, greater innovation, and long-term efficiencies. But because of several reasons, many governments are unable to identify, prepare, structure, and manage a successful and sustainable PPP. This situation results in many failed and cancelled projects, straining infrastructure development and public budgets even more. Time and again, the absence of a solid understanding of and experience in PPPs are cited as the cause.

That’s why the Inter-American Development Bank Group, the World Bank Group, and other multilateral development banks (MDBs) have joined forces to transform PPP practice worldwide with the new Certified PPP Professional (CP3P) credential and the PPP Certification Guide. This new effort outlines and defines a core set of skills needed by all PPP project teams and offers a common language for the PPP process that paves the way for shared understanding and appropriate expectations. It standardizes institutions’ approaches, covers topics evenly, and assures a sustainable delivery structure, enhancing the professionalism of PPP practitioners across sectors and regions.   

The PPP certification program, and innovation of the Asian Development Bank (ADB), European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD), Inter-American Development Bank (IDBG), Islamic Development Bank (IsDB), Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), the World Bank Group (WBG), funded by Public-Private Infrastructure Advisory Facility (PPIAF) and delivered by APMG International, is being launched and made available worldwide at PPPAmericas, an international conference that focuses on innovative trends in public-private partnerships (PPPs) in Latin America and the Caribbean. This year, PPPAmericas will take place on June 20 – 22 in Santiago, Chile. The launch of this program marks an important milestone in the IDBG and the EBRD´s efforts to create a knowledge-based capacity building initiative. 

Certification and credentials

To earn the CP3P designation, a candidate needs to successfully pass three levels of exams based on materials in the PPP Certification Guide: specifically, the Foundation, Preparation and Execution of PPPs. The PPP Certification Guide is an encyclopedic reference manual exhaustive in scope, meticulous in coverage, and unwavering in its attention to detail. It stands apart from other PPP source materials because ADB, EBRD, IDBG, IsDB, MIF and WBG hand-picked a core team of authors, along with 80 peer reviewers, who wrote and reviewed each section. These writers and reviewers are PPP experts with both recent and long-term experience in private sector and public sector transactions across industries and regions. Their broad and deep contributions to the PPP Certification Guide provide readers with access to materials, case studies, and other information that in some cases has never been published before. 

When IDBG banded together with other MDBs to support the PPP Certification Program, committing time and expertise, it did so believing that emerging markets and developing economies cannot afford to lose any more time in achieving their potential through PPPs. This approach to PPP education and training is a critical step forward that will usher in an era of even more effective, wide-ranging, and sustainable PPPs around the world.

About the authors

Leopoldo Montañez serves as Operational Advisory to the Infrastructure and Energy Department of the IDBG, and has 30 years of experience in the infrastructure sector, in areas of public policy, regulatory and institutional frameworks, financing, and infrastructure project management. In the public sector in Colombia was Deputy Minister of Energy, Chairman of the Electricity and Gas Regulatory Commission, Head of the Energy Division of the National Planning Department, and Member of several energy sector boards. In the private sector was President of the Colombian Natural Gas Association, and President of the International Gas Transportation Company. He also worked at the World Bank as Senior Energy Specialist with projects in several Latin American countries. Leopoldo is an Electrical Engineer from the National University of Colombia, with Electric Power Management studies in Japan, and Master in Energy Studies at the University of Sheffield, UK.

Jyoti Bisbey is the Program leader of the PPP Certification Program on behalf of the MDBs (ADB, EBRD, IADB via MIF, IsDB, WBG and PPIAF).  She sits in the PPP Unit of the World Bank Group and is based in Washington DC. Jyoti is currently leading the dialogue on PPPs in Kazakhstan and Tajikistan from the World Bank Group and previously in Brazil, Vietnam, Russia, Uruguay, Mozambique, Madagascar, Kenya and Ethiopia.  Her work focusses on infrastructure project financing, financial viability assessments and structuring of PPP options. Her recent achievements have been defining the private capital mobilization/catalyzation among development community; launching the Global PPP Certification program −‘CP3P’; financial recovery planning for Vietnam Electricity and; structuring PPPs in Irrigation toolkit. Jyoti specializes in Infrastructure Finance with an MBA from George Washington University and Mathematics in undergraduate, she has over 10 years of work experience in project finance transactions and PPPs.

The IDB and the ”la Caixa” Banking Foundation co-finance two new socioeconomic development projects in Colombia

The IDB and the ”la Caixa” Banking Foundation co-finance two new socioeconomic development projects in Colombia

December 14, 2015
By
María Paula Brito Rozas

 

  • The Manager of the Inter-American Development Bank Office of Outreach and Partnerships, Bernardo Guillamon; and the Director general of the ”la Caixa” Banking Foundation, Jaume Giró, signed a new partnership agreement in Washington to contribute to socioeconomic progress in Colombia. 
  • The agreement includes an investment of 500,000 euros from the la Caixa" Banking Foundation. The IDB will provide resources in the form of a loan. Both inputs will support two rural projects in generating new employment opportunities and improving the living conditions of Colombia’s  most vulnerable people.  
  • Both projects will be implemented under the IDB’s Social Entrepreneurship Program, administered by the Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF). The ”la Caixa” Banking Foundation will participate through its International Cooperation Program. 

 

Washington, December 11,  2015.- The Manager of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Office of Outreach and Partnerships, Bernardo Guillamon; and the director general of the ”la Caixa” Banking Foundation, Jaume Giró, have signed a joint agreement to support two new socioeconomic development projects in Colombia. This agreement renews a collaboration that began in December 2012 and funded two projects with the IDB’s Social Entrepreneurship Program (SEP) in Ecuador.

The two new projects to be carried out in Colombia are the result of a selection process launched in February 2015 to fund rural initiatives aimed at creating new job opportunities and improving the living conditions of people with limited resources. 

The project Social and Entrepreneurial Strengthening of the Women’s Coffee Growers Association of Western Huila will provide loans and technical assistance to the Cadefihuila Cooperative, in partnership with the Cooperative University of Colombia. Thanks to this initiative at least 250 women producers and their families will increase the quality and productivity of their coffee crops, and improve their environmental management and marketing practices.

The project Access to Finance and Entrepreneurial Development in Rural Nariño, will provide loan and technical cooperation resources to the microfinance NGO Nariño Business and Future Corporation (Contactar), in partnership with the Association for Rural Development. The aim is to improve the living conditions and food security of at least 600 farming families in the region. With technical assistance and access to green and sustainable agricultural credit, they may implement new agro-ecological systems.

Both projects will be implemented under the IDB’s Social Entrepreneurship Program, administered by the Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF). The "la Caixa" Banking Foundation will participate through its International Cooperation Program.

About the IDB

The Inter-American Development Bank is devoted to improving lives. Established in 1959, the IDB is a leading source of long-term financing for economic, social and institutional development in Latin America and the Caribbean. The IDB also conducts cutting-edge research and provides policy advice, technical assistance and training to public and private sector clients throughout the region.

About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, is funded by 39 countries and supports private sector-led development benefiting low-income populations and the poor—their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory—experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks to build and support successful micro, small, and medium business models. Learn more at www.fomin.org 

About la Caixa" Banking Foundation 

The "la Caixa" Banking Foundation is committed to helping eradicate poverty in low-income countries. It supports innovative, productive and job-creating initiatives in partnership with more than 70 Spanish and international organizations. 
The "la Caixa" International Cooperation Program was founded in  1997 and since then has supported 578 development and humanitarian aid projects in 62 countries, with contributions of over 122 million euros, as well as promotion of training and awareness in Spain.

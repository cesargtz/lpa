FOMIN and FUCREA support sustainable agriculture in Uruguay

FOMIN and FUCREA support sustainable agriculture in Uruguay

May 27, 2013
By
Claudia Moreno

On May 27, 2013, the Uruguayan Federation of Regional Agricultural Experimentation (FUCREA) and the Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, will sign a $ 1.8 million agreement to launch the Conservation of Natural Capital in New Agriculture project. The project will allow 400 small and medium agricultural producers to develop land management plans for their facilities that will be both environmentally and financially sustainable. It is expected that an area of at least 250,000 hectares will be cultivated under sustainable practices, with the benefits that this entails for producers and rural livelihoods.

 
This will be possible through the development of environmental and financial simulation models , and the generation of indicators and planning tools that can facilitate decision making for producers when they plant their crops or negotiate the leases of their lands with third parties.

The expansion of agriculture in Uruguay and other countries in the region has had significant effects on export performance and a spillover effect on rural and small town populations. However, in certain situations, evidence shows that dominant agricultural practices are not sustainable from a natural capital perspective.

FUCREA shares the vision that natural capital conservation has to go hand in hand with dynamic growth of agriculture as a source of income and development opportunities for producers and their families. This project is expected to develop and test alternative sustainable models that are profitable for companies, such as integration of other crops and “agricultural rotation” Practices.  The tools generated by the project will be managed through various instruments such as classroom activities, a web platform and mobile applications. It is expected that the information and knowledge generated in the project will be shared with other producer organizations at a regional level.

"The proposal is a contribution that will align with the natural capital care plans of the Government of Uruguay and its specialized agencies.  We expect to replicate this innovative model in Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay and Bolivia, to the extent that the need to address the impacts on social and environmental sustainability is observed across the region,” said Ana Castillo, MIF Specialist. “With FUCREA we have a reliable partner with extensive experience in the field of agricultural knowledge management,” she added.

The signing ceremony will be held in FUCREA’s headquarters in the city of Montevideo and will be headed by Mr. Juan José Taccone, Representative of the Inter-American Development Bank in Uruguay and Mr. Romeo Volonté, president of FUCREA.

About the MIF The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), funded by 39 donors, supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models. More information at www.fomin.org.

About FUCREA

The Uruguayan Federation of Regional Agricultural Experimentation (FUCREA) is a nonprofit organization formed by groups of farmers with 45 years of experience in knowledge management and social capital formation in the Uruguayan agriculture sector. It is composed of more than 600 producer families who work in the exchange of ideas and experiences among peers with the purpose of responding to the technical, economic and human needs of businesses. The vast majority of its groups are composed of family businesses, representing a "vanguard" in terms of technology and management of their firms. Find out more about FUCREA in www.fucrea.org

Harnessing the power of technology to improve youth employability in Suriname

Harnessing the power of technology to improve youth employability in Suriname

September 05, 2013
By
Georg Neumann

 

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, will provide $988,000 to the Back Lot Foundation to support its youth employment training program.  The Back Lot is an organization based in Paramaribo, Suriname that uses film and media to empower youth. The Interchurch Organization for Development Cooperation (ICCO) will provide an additional $1.5 million for the project, which aims to increase the employability of 1,240 youth through an information and communications technology (ICT)-driven training model.  

Suriname has a large youth population. The overall unemployment rate for youth ages 15 to 24 is about 22 percent, compared to a national unemployment rate of 8 percent, with higher youth unemployment in the interior of the country. Youth in Suriname, particularly those from poor and low-income households, face significant barriers to employability, largely stemming from a lack of academic support and limited access to educational resources such as qualified teachers, books, and computers. Furthermore, the skills taught in technical or vocational training programs often do not match the needs of local employers.

Through a mix of online, face-to-face, and on-the-job training, the project will help youth acquire the basic skills needed for entry-level jobs across sectors, taking advantage of their innate ability with technology to further develop their ICT skills and keep them engaged with the program. Emphasis will be placed on building youth life skills (communication, teamwork, responsibility, etc.) using technology, media, and film as a “hook” to maintain their interest and minimize attrition. The combination of in-person classroom instruction and on-the-job training at participating NGOs, community-based organizations, or companies will help youth to apply new skills in a practical work environment. During the on-the-job training, youth will also participate in distance learning assignments.

The Back Lot will work together with at least 50 NGOs, community-based organizations, and companies to ensure that training course content meets the needs of on-the-job training hosts and potential employers and to select youth participants.  The training curricula include: Life Skills and Basic ICT, Project Management, ICT and Media, and Learning to Work. Rural youth will be offered all basic curricula, while urban youth will be offered the streamlined Learning to Work curriculum.

Additionally the project will develop a “how to” guide for training purposes that will combine the training curricula, the methodology, and materials for trainers and youth to serve as a model for other organizations in the Caribbean.

About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank Group, is funded by 39 donors and supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models. More information at www.fomin.org.

About The Back Lot

The Back Lot is a foundation created in 2002 by The Back Lot Holding N.V, a private cinema company based in Paramaribo, Suriname. The Back Lot has experience using film and media to engage children and youth; particularly in the production of film festivals, documentaries, education programs, and children’s news programs which are shown in the country’s interior and in Paramaribo on different subjects such as HIV and child labor. Through these productions, The Back Lot has forged partnerships with the private sector and understands how to reach out to youth and spark their interest in learning using media and film. The Back Lot has experience managing projects with other donors such as UNICEF, the UNDP, and the Pan-American Health Organization. More information at www.thebacklot.sr

About ICCO

The Interchurch Organization for Development Cooperation (ICCO) is a Dutch NGO founded in 1964 with the mission to work towards a world in which people can live in dignity and well-being; a world without poverty and injustice. ICCO works in 44 countries in Africa, Asia, and Latin America.  More information at www.icco-international.com.

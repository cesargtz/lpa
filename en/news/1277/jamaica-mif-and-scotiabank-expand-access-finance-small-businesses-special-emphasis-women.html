Jamaica: MIF and Scotiabank to expand access to finance for small businesses, with special emphasis on women-owned firms

August 29, 2012
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), in partnership with Scotiabank, has approved a project under its Line of Activity for Promoting Small Enterprise Financing that will increase access to finance for 300 small businesses in Jamaica, with special emphasis on women-led enterprises. About 40% of small businesses in Jamaica identify access to finance as a major constraint to the development of their businesses. Additionally, the project will provide capacity building and mentoring services to selected businesses in order to prepare them to receive business and credit risk ratings, which will further facilitate their access to loans provided by Scotiabank Jamaica. The project will ensure that at least 30% of the businesses selected to participate in this training will be owned by women.

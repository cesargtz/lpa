Recognizing excellence in Caribbean microfinance

Recognizing excellence in Caribbean microfinance

November 11, 2013
By
Ryan Tang

Award winners Jurgen Boodie (left), Yolanda Gomez and Marcus James

On the evening of October 31, 2013, microfinance stakeholders from around the Caribbean gathered at the Courtleigh Hotel in Kingston, Jamaica to participate in the second annual Citi Microentrepreneurship Awards for the Caribbean, sponsored by Citi Foundation and the Multilateral Investment Fund under the auspices of the Caribbean Microfinance Capacity-Building Project II. The Excellence in Microentrepreneurship in Suriname award was presented to Mr. Jurgen Boodie, owner of Restaurant JiJi’s, the Excellence in Microfinance Product Innovation award was won by Access Financial Services in Jamaica, and La Inmaculada Credit Union in Belize emerged as the winner of the Excellence in Microfinance Transparency award. As a few of the speakers noted, there is something special about gathering microfinance stakeholders in one place and recognizing the accomplishments of the best.

A Microfinance Start-up Becomes Front-runner, Client Protection, and ‘Very, Very Good Sauce’
Watching the video of Mr. Jurgen Boodie, we were inspired by his dedication and passion for his restaurant and won over by his enthusiasm for his “very, very good sauce!” which he plans to bottle and bring to market in the near future. Mr. Boodie represents the many passionate and talented entrepreneurs out there that can benefit from microcredit as they build their success.

Marcus James, founder and CEO of Access Financial Services in Jamaica, was praised for his drive, leadership and entrepreneurial spirit in growing Access into one of the largest microfinance institutions in Jamaica. While many in the region previously thought of microfinance as a program for NGOs and government, Access has shown microfinance’s great potential as a business. Founded in 2000, it now serves over 15,000 active loan clients through a network of 15 branches.

Caribbean MicroFinance Alliance Board Chair Ramesh Persaud described the leadership of Yolanda Gomez, CEO of La Inmaculada Credit Union, well when he stated that Yolanda’s respect and concern for those around her flows from her to the La Inmaculada staff team and on to the credit union’s members. Her receiving the transparency award reflects La Inmaculada’s concern for client protection and client education, one of many ways it excels in serving its members.

We gathered to recognize the award winners and finalists but, it was also an excellent opportunity to recognize the accomplishments and growth of the Caribbean microfinance sector as a whole. The Caribbean MicroFinance Alliance, formed in ­­2011, with support from Carib-Cap II, has grown to 23 members. Twenty institutions are registered on the Microfinance Information Exchange (MIX) whereas a few years ago, only a couple were. Through capacity-building, networking and hard work, MFIs in the Caribbean now know how to provide microcredit loans much more efficiently and with lower arrears. There has been a marked improvement in their internal processes and MFI client outreach of participants in Carib-Cap II has grown by 44% per year.

Looking at the awards ceremony itself, two notable additions to last year’s ceremony in Barbados were the performance of a 16-year-old Jamaican professional violinist named Jovani Williams, and the cool, rich voice of Michael Anthony Cuffe, Jamaican radio and television talk show host, as the Master of Ceremonies. The bar for the Citi Microentrepreneurship Awards has been set high and I look forward to watching the growth of this awards program along with the growth of the Caribbean microfinance sector.

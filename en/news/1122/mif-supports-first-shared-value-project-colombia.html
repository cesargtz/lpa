The MIF supports the first Shared Value project in Colombia

The MIF supports the first Shared Value project in Colombia

June 20, 2013
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank Group, has approved a $1,615,100 grant to support a project in Colombia, implemented by the ANDI Foundation, which will develop methodologies to create and incorporate businesses owned by poor and low-income people into the parts of anchor company value chains that generate the most value.

Even though microenterprises owned by poor and low-income populations have been linked to anchor firms in Colombia, they generally operate in segments of the value chain which require very basic skills and generate low income. Thus, the microenterprises’ growth potential, development of robust business capabilities and value creation for the anchor firms have all been limited.

As suppliers or distributors of products and services with little value added, their growth depends on the anchor firms’ business performance, as well as the structure and governance of their value chains. Additionally, the lack of methodologies to convert microenterprises into small businesses means that even those with high value and growth potential do not have the tools they need to take advantage of opportunities when presented. 

The project will finance specialized consulting services and bring its own technical expertise to support the creation of new businesses in more sophisticated activities. The project will work with anchor firms’ value chains and explore scaling opportunities through clusters or a group of firms in the same or complementary industries and upgrade existing microenterprises that are already part of value chains. The project will use selected anchor firms’ value chains as a vehicle to accelerate existing microbusinesses or incubate higher value micro and small businesses, using an approach known as “shared value,” by which economic value is created in a way that also generates value for society by addressing its needs and challenges.
 

In the pilot phase, the project will upgrade approximately 120 microenterprises and create 15 new businesses, ultimately improving the livelihoods of 1,500 poor and low-income individuals. ANDI Foundation I will transfer methodologies to 20 institutions working with business creation and strengthening of microenterprises, creating new tools to develop the potential of microenterprises. Thus, the ultimate goal is to get microenterprises joining the ranks of the so-called "small and growing businesses" and generating extraordinary value for both businesses owned the poor and for anchor companies. It hopes to establish a new paradigm where poverty is not synonymous with subsistence microenterprises.

 


About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), funded by 39 donors, supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models. More information at www.fomin.org

 


About the ANDI Foundation

The ANDI Foundation is an institution created in 1997 by large firms affiliated with the National Association of Businesses of Colombia (ANDI). ANDI is the most important business association in Colombia, representing medium and large businesses, with 15 offices around the country and 22 industry-specific chambers housed within its structure. ANDI is the regional focal point for the UN Global Compact, and a member of the National Committee on Inclusive Business. The objective of the ANDI Foundation is to become the "hub" for shared value in Colombia. More information at www.andi.com.co/

NEO: One year later

NEO: One year later

April 18, 2013
By
Elena Heredero Rodriguez

NEO - New Employment Opportunities for Youth- marked one year since its launch at last year’s Summit of the Americas in Cartagena, Colombia. The implementation team (the MIF, IYF and the IDB) has had a frantic year of planning and preparation to begin its execution, but we’ve also seen significant progress on all fronts of developing this ambitious initiative.

What is NEO?

As you may recall, NEO is a partnership between public and private actors and NGOs to promote the entry of 1 million vulnerable young people into the labor markets of 10 countries in Latin America and the Caribbean. The MIF, the IDB's Social Sector and the International Youth Foundation are the drivers and co-managers of the initiative, which also includes 5 large companies: Arcos Dorados, Caterpillar, CEMEX, Microsoft and Walmart. The company Manpower has also joined in an advisory role. Currently NEO is in the first phase of implementation (2012-2017), with an intermediate goal of reaching half a million young people.

What have we achieved since the launch?

Operationalizing the focus on collective impact . What do we mean by this? At the heart of NEO is working in partnership with stakeholders from different sectors, all of which have come together from day one to solve a social problem—in this case, youth unemployment, underemployment and informal employment— with a long-term, sustainable vision. The five key ingredients of this collaboration are: 1) having a strategic, common plan for common change, 2) articulating different yet mutually reinforcing activities for each of the actors, 3) establishing a uniform system for measuring results, 4) sustaining continuous communication, 5) organizing leadership to maintain collaboration and execution of the plan. To achieve this long-range systemic impact, the MIF, the IDB and IYF have developed new systems for providing technical and financial support for this approach in the countries.

Launching activities in 5 countries: Mexico, the Dominican Republic, Colombia, Brazil and Panama. In Mexico we began implementing the NEO collective impact approach in the State of Nuevo Leon, in the north of the country. There are five entities that are leading local efforts now to develop its strategic plan for change: CEMEX, Conalep Nuevo Leon (a technical education institution), FEMSA, Banamex and Manpower. This "promoting group" is engaged in a participatory strategic planning process to achieve the systemic change plan as mentioned, as well as plans for investment, structuring the partnership and implementing the initiative. Another initiative being started is a Free Education program with the Confederation of Industries of Brazil, a program with large-scale potential that was developed in parallel with NEO. It seeks to develop an online platform to teach Portuguese, mathematics and "soft" skills and to develop learning paths. At this moment, the platform is in a beta version and we are working on building a partnership with major national entities, such as the SESI/SENAI  network, the Federation of State Industries, and several ministries. In the other three countries we are in the early stages of exploration and planning.

Knowledge transfer of and results framework . The NEO team is finalizing a quality standards guide for training institutions and employment agencies, to be applied to providers of such services. We will begin testing the tool in Mexico and then extend it to other countries. We are also beginning to develop regional information and monitoring system for NEO. It will have a set of key indicators, common to the various NEO country initiatives, and with accommodate the addition of baseline data, results and impacts to allow for effective analysis and management that facilitates learning, decision-making and accountability.

What do we expect for the coming year?

  • Launch at least 3 new NEO initiatives in three countries (possibly Mexico, the Dominican Republic and Panama)
  • Start a process of exploring and strategic planning in 3 additional countries (likely Peru, Chile and Uruguay)
  • Develop a new guide for work relationship to improve services from different providers
  • Design and start implementing the regional information and monitoring system, accompanied by an agenda of learning and knowledge generation
  • Establish a brand identity that allows us to clearly communicate the program’s goals and continue to attract new members.

Our commitment from now on is to be much more consistent in our communications, with both our current partners, and with the online community. We think that in this way the NEO initiative, which is just now learning to crawl, can tomorrow walk hand in hand with new fellow adventurers.

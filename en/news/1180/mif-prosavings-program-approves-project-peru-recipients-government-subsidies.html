MIF ProSavings Program approves project in Peru for recipients of government subsidies

MIF ProSavings Program approves project in Peru for recipients of government subsidies

January 23, 2013
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group has approved a $189,800 grant to support the project “Savings for All” (Ahorro para Todos). This is the fifth project to be executed under the ProSavings program framework and the first to take place in Peru. Savings for All will be executed by Caja Rural de Ahorro y Credito Nuestra Gente or Caja Nuestra Gente (CNG). This project is financed by the MIF, CNG, the Citi Foundation, AusAID, and the Development Bank of Latin America (CAF).

 
This project seeks to promote the development and use of planned and liquid savings products targeted at recipients of government subsidies. This pilot initiative will cover three departments located within the South and Central Andean areas of the country, identified as areas with high poverty levels. A business strategy will be implemented in these locations, which will be tailored to the needs and characteristics of conditional cash transfer (CCT) recipients, mainly from the JUNTOS Program. The strategy will also incorporate savings group methodologies.

Since 2005, the Government of Peru has carried out a program of conditional cash transfers, JUNTOS, through the Presidency of the Council of Ministers. The program provides direct cash transfers to mothers representing households living in poverty or extreme poverty in both rural and urban areas in 14 regions and 116 provinces . By the end of 2011 the program reported benefiting a total of 492,871 households*, to which bimonthly payments were madein the amount of US$73.50 and were conditional on the fulfillment of health, education, nutrition, and identity verification requirements.

The rationale behind the “Savings for All” project is rooted in previous experiences with CCT recipients in Peru, which have revealed that this population, despite exhibiting amongst the lowest levels of income, has the capacity to save if offered products that suit their needs and financial reality. Currently, concerns remain about the level of financial sustainability associated with extending savings products to the low-income population. Therefore, Savings for All will focus on generating financially viable strategies that are able to benefit both the target population as well as the financial institution. 

About the ProSavings Program

The purpose of the MIF’s ProSavings program is to expand the availability of financial products and services targeted to low-income populations in Latin America and the Caribbean, through the development of between 10 and 14 projects in which financial intermediaries will implement business models for developing liquid and planned savings products that respond to the needs and characteristics of populations receiving conditional cash transfers, accompanied by financial education and alternative distribution channels.

About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), funded by 39 donors, supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models. About Caja Nuestra Gente

Caja Rural de Ahorro y Credito Nuestra Gente (CNG) was established on 4 September 1994. It is part of the financial system, takes deposits from the public, and specializes in lending to micro, small, and medium-sized enterprises in rural areas.

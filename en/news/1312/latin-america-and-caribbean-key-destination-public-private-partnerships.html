Latin America and the Caribbean: A key destination for Public Private Partnerships

Latin America and the Caribbean: A key destination for Public Private Partnerships

March 18, 2012
By
David Bloomgarden

In recent months, there have been interesting developments in the world of Public Private Partnerships which will affect how governments manage these programs in the future. I had the opportunity to attend the PPP Days 2012  in Geneva the last week of February to discuss with representatives from over 90 countries. 

Although Latin America and the Caribbean were outnumbered by European, African and Asian countries, the region stood out in terms of its global importance in the PPP market worldwide. Between 1990 and 2011, it accounted for 31% of PPPs globally. Latin America and the Caribbean is also the largest destination for PPP investment in the world. We at the Multilateral Investment Fund have been a partner from the beginning for many of the most significant PPP programs in the region including Chile, Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico as well as new and promising PPP programs such as Uruguay – all of which were represented at the conference. 
 
Key issues of the discussion centered on:
  • renewed pressure of the costs of financing,
  • shortened tenors in many private markets, and
  • transparency, governance and accountability.
In terms of finance, Latin America and the Caribbean, according to the Private Participation in Infrastructure (PPI) Project Database, outpaced the world in total PPP investment commitments of $10.3 billion in the first half of 2011. Part of this success is attributable to the increased role of public finance in PPPs. In terms of accountability and transparency, Peru and Chile both showed high levels of disclosure relating to contract information and performance along Minas Gerais which was selected to speak at the panel on transparency. 
 
The region was recognized for its increasing knowledge and experience. The World Bank Institute organized a meeting between several African countries and attendees from Latin America and the Caribbean to discuss a proposed knowledge exchange program. Both the countries and the MIF consider this as a useful way to promote and exchange best practices globally. In addition, the PPP unit in the Colombian Ministry of Finance and the Department of Planning produced a new guide for best practices in PPPs, and the PPP unit in Brazil at the Ministry of Planning is producing a case study guide on the Brazilian experience, both of which have been financed by the MIF and will be highlighted on the MIF PPP website soon. 
 
This year, the MIF will be looking at innovations for green PPPs. 
 
Through the National Program for the Institutional Development of Public-Private Partnerships, the MIF has worked with the Federal Ministry of Planning to support PPPs in Brazil. With Brazil accounting for nearly three quarters of all conserved areas on the planet, last fall the Ministry entered into a historic agreement with the Ministry of Environment to develop PPPs for the improved management of federal Conservation Units, whether through the preservation of natural lands, facilitating improved visitation, environmental education or scientific research. Resources from the MIF program will be used for feasibility studies needed to structure the PPPs and more in-depth research on “green PPPs” in partnership with the Economists Intelligence Unit. 
 
In a future blog, I look forward to sharing with you the results of this research on how PPP models can be used to achieve environmental benefits. I would welcome your comments and suggestions.

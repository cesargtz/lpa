The MIF and the Semana Foundation partner to revive the Colombian economy in post-conflict areas

The MIF and the Semana Foundation partner to revive the Colombian economy in post-conflict areas

December 05, 2012
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the IDB Group, approved a US$ 2 million grant for a project that will help the Semana Foundation of Colombia to generate sustainable incomes for 1,000 families returned to post-conflict regions. The families, displaced by violence during the country’s armed conflict, are mostly headed by women and live in the town of El Salado and two other parts of the Montes de Maria area.

This will be achieved through the implementation of a Local Economic Development strategy focused on the development of micro, small and medium enterprises through public-private interaction and strengthening the territory’s competitive factors. Specifically, the project will link families with large companies and with dynamic and highly productive markets. The MIF will support the Semana Foundation in bringing together private and public actors, and will strengthen the competitiveness of the three post-conflict zones according to identified regional demand.

At present, 400 localities in Colombia that are in the process of resettlement have income levels classified as high poverty. The instability in these resettlement areas is a result of many factors. One of this is the temporary nature of the financial compensation received by the inhabitants of these areas, which do not generate sustainable income to enable them to take advantage of market opportunities and establish stable livelihoods.

The project will strengthen public-private governance by conducting workshops on local economic development for 60 leaders from the public and private sectors of each of the three targeted regions. In addition, the initiative will train beneficiaries in management of local development through workshops in leadership and citizen participation. These activities will be key to the design and implementation of an ongoing, permanent participation plan in each territory that includes target groups, local authorities and executing agencies.

Finally, the project will develop a culture of partnership through the creation of at least 6 community properties, such as storage facilities, drying plants, bank machines, cooling tanks, collective purchase of supplies, and development of a distinctive brand to foster business competitiveness in the locations included in the project. It is estimated that the project will build employment and self-employment opportunities for 450 people who will work in the production units created through the project.

Since 2009, the Semana Foundation has developed a model of physical reconstruction and regional social capital, in collaboration with an alliance of 70 private companies in El Salado.

About Semana Foundation

The Semana Foundation is part of Semana Publications, one of Colombia’s most influential publishing firms.  It is a non-profit organization whose main objective is to work for social inclusion in Colombia. The foundation has developed a model for physical reconstruction and rebuilding of social capital in the region of El Salado through public-private cooperation, including NGOs, international cooperation organizations, and civil society. Currently, thanks to the establishment of a partnership with nearly 70 private companies, the community has improved access roads, primary medical care, improved housing and sanitary facilities, a community dining room tied to an early childhood education process, a project for recovery and access to land for growing crops, access to microfinance services, water systems, sewer systems, a wastewater treatment plant, and a rebuilt cultural center. Since April 2009, the foundation has established partnerships with 85 private and public companies to carry out its mission and has worked with IOM/USAID.

MIF, Guatemalan government join forces to promote public-private partnerships

MIF, Guatemalan government join forces to promote public-private partnerships

June 26, 2013
By
Georg Neumann

The Guatemalan National Agency of Alliances for the Development of Economic Infrastructure (Agencia Nacional de Alianzas para el Desarrollo de la Infraestructura Económica or ANADIE) and the Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, will launch a program to strengthen the capacity of the Guatemalan government and private sector to contract for infrastructure and services using public-private partnership (PPP) mechanisms. If implemented correctly, PPPs could serve to fund needed infrastructure through private sector financing, thus improving the country's infrastructure and increasing its potential to promote economic growth, leading to the development of Guatemala.

In 2010, the Guatemalan government passed the Partnerships for Economic Infrastructure Development Law, in order to establish a more favorable environment for private participation in developing the country's infrastructure. The law established ANADIE as the country’s specialized entity for PPPs, with responsibility for structuring and procurement of PPP projects, advising institutions that contract with the state, and managing PPP projects, together with other institutions.

The MIF will contribute to the program by hiring expert advisors to train key public and private sector actors involved in PPPs, support promotion of and strategic communication about the new PPP program, and to assist with the planning of future PPP projects, through the development of standard legal documents and the definition of business models for potential projects that have been identified by ANADIE.

Through its participation in the program, the Ministry of Public Finance will increase its ability to assess the fiscal impact of PPP projects, which is key to the management of any government liabilities that could result from obligations to concessionaires. The MIF program will strengthen ANADIE’s capacity to develop PPP projects from initial conception to contracting and contract management. Both parties believe that working together will contribute to the development of major infrastructure projects as well as a significant increase in private investment.

The program will be funded through the MIF Regional Public-Private Partnerships Advisory Services Program, also known as New Frontiers in PPPs. The program is a five-year, $8 million initiative, co-financed and implemented in coordination with the Infrastructure and Environment Sector (INE) of the IDB, that will provide technical assistance to countries in the Latin American and Caribbean region that are new to public-private partnerships. The program is expected to build the capacity of national and sub-national governments and help them identify and structure projects likely to attract private sector funding. The program also aims to create channels for learning and sharing experiences among countries in the region and beyond.

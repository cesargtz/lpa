Supporting women entrepreneurs in Uruguay

Supporting women entrepreneurs in Uruguay

January 24, 2013
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, has approved a US$742,000.0 technical cooperation project to promote the creation and development of women-led enterprises in Uruguay. The project builds on the work of the Uruguayan chapter of Endeavor, a leading global organization promoting high impact entrepreneurship, which has performed an in-depth diagnosis of the barriers Uruguayan women face in starting and running businesses as part of its program for women entrepreneurs. With MIF support, the new project aims to tackle the identified barriers by improving access to advisory services for women entrepreneurs whose businesses are at various stages of development, and for women interested in creating and developing businesses. 

Women with very small businesses or those interested in developing their businesses will have access to awareness-building conferences, specialized training, and networking events. For women entrepreneurs interested in growing their businesses, the project will offer mentorships (individual and group), business plan development consulting services, and personalized advice on obtaining access to financing and addressing the challenges faced by this type of enterprise. Additionally, entrepreneurs with dynamic businesses will be able to participate in Endeavor’s annual international selection panel, which provides access to a network of successful and recognized leaders and entrepreneurs from various industries, and to investors who can help enhance the growth of their businesses so they can have an impact at the national and international level.

According to the Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM), the most pressing problem for Uruguay is not the number of enterprises but their quality in terms of growth, exports, and innovation. This is more pronounced in the case of women-led businesses, which are primarily microenterprises and are concentrated in sectors where they have lower growth potential than their male-led counterparts. Consequently, while women entrepreneurs are not excluded from formal business development services, the offerings of these services do not match women’s specific needs for training, advice, access to contact networks, and financing to develop their businesses.

In 2010, Endeavor Uruguay launched the program “Women Entrepreneurs Breaking Down Barriers,” which raised the awareness of more than 800 women interested in creating and developing businesses. As part of the program, a Survey of Women Entrepreneurs on Gender Barriers in Uruguay was produced, which revealed that women entrepreneurs in Uruguay, in addition to having difficulties reconciling family and professional life, face negative stereotypes, lack positive attitudes regarding their own personal abilities, and are not aware of success stories of women enterprises that could stimulate their entrepreneurial interests. Building on the experience of this program, the project aims to expand its scope and include specific training, mentorships, and personalized advisory services for women entrepreneurs with micro, small, and medium-sized enterprises .

For the program’s execution, Endeavor Uruguay also sought the support of Organización de Mujeres Emprendedoras del Uruguay [Organization of Women Entrepreneurs of Uruguay] (OMEU), the country’s only network of women entrepreneurs, which will allow beneficiaries to gain access to the Les Femmes Chefs d’Entreprises Mondiales [World Association of Women Entrepreneurs] (FCEM) network and remain in contact with, and receive support from, FCEM members after program completion.

For its part, the MIF has contributed to the development of a local entrepreneurial ecosystem in Uruguay through various interventions:  creating the Ingenio Incubator (LATU) and Endeavor Uruguayan offices; the Emprender Program, which led to the creation of the Red Emprender, and the Corporate Entrepreneurship program, among others. However, women’s participation in these programs has been low.

About Endeavor Uruguay

 

 

Endeavor Uruguay is the local subsidiary of Endeavor Inc., a nonprofit organization created in 2000 with MIF assistance and financed through contributions from multilateral agencies, companies, and individuals that believe in the ability of entrepreneurs to multiply economic, social, and cultural changes. In the past two years, Endeavor implemented the program “Mujeres Emprendedoras Derribando Barreras” [Women Entrepreneurs Breaking Down Barriers], with the support of Sabre Holding and the United States Embassy. This program gave motivational and networking seminars led by Red Endeavor mentors and raised the awareness of more than 800 women interested in creating and developing their businesses.

About Organización de Mujeres Emprendedoras del Uruguay (OMEU)

 

Organización de Mujeres Emprendedoras del Uruguay [Organization of Women Entrepreneurs of Uruguay] is a nonprofit civil organization founded in 2009 and devoted to women entrepreneurs. It currently includes 50 active members and its board of directors consists of 10 women with proven track records in the local business community. OMEU is a full member of the Les Femmes Chefs d´Entreprises Mondiales [World Association of Women Entrepreneurs] (FCEM) network, which pioneered the practice of bringing together women business leaders from around the world. Created in France in 1945, FCEM now has a presence in more than 80 countries, where it promotes links among women who share an interest in the entrepreneurial spirit. Approved amount: US$ 742,280.00

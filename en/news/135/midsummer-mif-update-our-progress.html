Midsummer at the MIF: An update on our progress

Midsummer at the MIF: An update on our progress

July 20, 2016
By
Brigit Helms

I recently marked 6 months as general manager of the MIF. Since I last updated you on our work, still more has happened, so it seems like a good time to share some of the latest developments with you.

New areas of focus

Last month, our Donors Committee approved our new business plan, which sharpens the focus of our work. Over the last two decades, the MIF has worked in a wide range of issues, which has given us the opportunity to learn a lot and work with many interesting partners, but on the other hand has spread us thin and limited the impact we have been able to have. As we told you in a recent issue of our newsletter, we are now organizing ourselves around three main topics: Inclusive Cities, Climate-Smart Agriculture, and the Knowledge Economy. As I mentioned before, the process of selecting these topics involved the entire MIF staff, colleagues from other parts of the IDB Group, and experts in the region and throughout the world of international development. These are all topics of key importance to the future of Latin America and the Caribbean, and there is a lot of enthusiasm around them among our staff and in the region alike. They are also well aligned with the top priorities of the IDB and IIC, giving us more opportunities to collaborate with colleagues from other parts of the IDB Group in sourcing, developing, executing, and scaling projects.

Filling the pipeline

So now that we have our new focus areas set, we are hard at work building a project portfolio that reflects them! Over the years, the way we approve and design projects had become more and more complicated, so we’ve been taking steps to streamline the process so we can jump into our new projects as quickly as possible. One way we’ve done this is by holding “pitch sessions” instead of long eligibility meetings – a bit like what you might see on “Shark Tank,” but hopefully a little friendlier. The team proposing a project has just 10 minutes to tell our management team about the idea, then the managers have just 10 minutes to ask questions, and finally the teams have 10 more minutes to respond. We then can give the team a thumbs-up or thumbs-down on the spot, and if it’s a yes they can then get right to work designing the project and readying it for donor approval. It’s been a lot of fun, and we already have over 40 potential projects that have cleared the pitch process. Colleagues from other parts of the Bank are getting involved as well—about three-quarters of the teams behind the approved pitches have included members from outside the MIF. IDB Group colleagues have also at times joined us to hear pitches and ask questions. Now we’re busy with the next stages of developing these projects, so stay tuned for a lot of new project announcements later this year.

Traveling the region

Perhaps the best part of the last few months is that I’ve finally had a chance to spend some quality time in the region. I attended two MIF conferences: Proadapt in Cartagena, Colombia in May, which was the first gathering dedicated to discussing private sector challenges and opportunities in climate change adaptation efforts; and PPPAmericas in Santiago, Chile in June, one of the MIF’s flagship events, focused on public-private partnerships, which are especially relevant to our Sustainable Cities area. Events like these are such a great opportunity to witness the MIF and IDB’s convening power in action, and meet with so many experts and innovators to hear about what’s working well in the region and what’s not—and where we should go next.


 

In and around these conferences, and in visits to Mexico, Argentina, and Paraguay, I have had the opportunity to meet an amazing array of current and potential partners. For instance, in Mexico, I met with the 14 crowdfunding platforms we’re working with to understand their opportunities and challenges; in Colombia I visited the new conference center in Barranquilla, part of an urban renovation initiative in that city; in Chile I met with Sistema B, one of the many social entrepreneurship innovators in that country; in Argentina I saw Fondation Forge in action, training thousands of underprivileged youth to join the modern workforce, and in Paraguay I strolled through Las Chacaritas, a slum where we’ll be working together with the IDB to offer revitalization and economic opportunity to the inhabitants.
 

The rest of the year promises to be equally exciting. In addition to attending Foromic in Jamaica in late October, I’ll be in Central America, South America, and the Caribbean before the year is out. Watch our Twitter account and newsletters for more details, I’ll be in touch!

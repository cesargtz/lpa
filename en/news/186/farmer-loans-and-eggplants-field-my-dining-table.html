Farmer loans and eggplants, from the field to my dining table

Farmer loans and eggplants, from the field to my dining table

October 02, 2017
By
Alejandro Escobar A.

 

It’s always an amazing experience when I meet people like Maria Lourdes, an eggplant and banana farmer based in Copan, Honduras. The temperature is almost 100 degrees out here at her farm. In my ignorance I ask her if this is the hot season? She smiles and responds: “…well its usually like this year-round!” With her boots well-grounded on the land, she explains how she started with a little over 10,000 square feet and now she has 2 acres (80,000 square feet) of eggplant grown with drip irrigation technology. She proudly tells us about the growth of her farm operation as her 5 employees stand silently behind her. As a single mother of 2, she works the land year-round, under the sun, managing her 5 full time employees and the other 20 or so she hires during harvest time. She also oversees the purchasing of all the inputs needed and after the harvest season, she also negotiates the sale of her production to a local exporter. What an amazing woman!! I ask her if she keeps some of the eggplants for her own consumption. She replies: “… of course!! I always check the quality of my product!”

Water supply is critical for her farm. She buys water from a local private water truck dispenser and gets the irrigation equipment and other inputs from FERTICA, a local agricultural firm which has initiated a micro credit program to reach small farmers like Maria Lourdes. She explains that a micro loan from a local microfinance organization at 30% interest rate would simply not work for her. The interest rate and the terms of the loan are not adequate for her needs. She states: “Financial institutions simply don’t understand farming”. FERTICA, on the other hand, offers her a much better rate (in the 7% to 10% range) and has worked out a credit payment scheme with the exporter who withholds the loan amount from the payment to her and other farmers like her. This allows for lower costs of credit management, underwriting and a lower risk of loss. Some 600 other farmers are working in this scheme with FERTICA. FERTICA has approached the MIF to work together with our experience in microfinance, to reach some 2000 additional farmers.

When I asked her if she knows where her eggplants are ultimately sold, she smiles: “… our exporter buys from me and some 30 other farmers and sells to three companies, but I forget their names!” The FERTICA manager steps in and clarifies, “…the exporter sells to Kroger, Giant and Wholefoods in North America.” At that moment I stop and ponder. And it strikes me. This woman is feeding me and my family! I make a gesture and smile to her... I reveal her what I just realized and she smiles and says... “Well then thanks for buying!” – to which I respond: “Thank you for growing my food!”

As we walk away from the farm and into the pick-up truck that brought us here, I look over to the FERTICA technician and I tell him... “This is why I love my work!!!” I get to meet the farmers who grow our food!”

Itau and the MIF formalize a program to support female entrepreneurship in Brazil

Itau and the MIF formalize a program to support female entrepreneurship in Brazil

January 29, 2013
By
Claudia Moreno

Sao Paulo, January 29, 2013 - The Multilateral Investment Fund, a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) Group, and Itau Unibanco have formalized a technical cooperation agreement to implement a project that aims to study the creation of credit policies, products and services specifically for women entrepreneurs in Brazil. The partnership, known as women entrepreneurshipBanking (weB), is unprecedented in Latin America and the Caribbean. Over a two year period, the project will identify the financial and nonfinancial needs of the targeted female audience, and will design products and establish relationships to develop projects and promote the stabilization of household income for that group. The idea to launch the weB program grew out of field tests, which identified specific characteristics of the development of women’s entrepreneurship. Women now occupy a prominent place in contributing to household income, adding to their parenting and household duties. Research on the topic demonstrated women business owners’ skills in multitasking, communicating with employees and suppliers, and willingness to plan, but at times they lack know-how. In this context, there has been an important growth of small businesses run by women, both in Latin America and the Caribbean in general, and Brazil in particular. "Women think, act and behave differently than men, so there is a need to consider new ways to serve this audience," said Andrea Pinotti, director of Itaú Unibanco. A study by Itaú shows that enterprises run by women only represent 22% of the bank's total portfolio, with greater participation in the trade sector. "We believe this is an opportunity to get to know this audience, which is gaining market representation, and learning from this project could be extended to other bank customers," added Pinotti. The project’s goals include supporting 1,500 women entrepreneurs operating in the franchise market, in small businesses in sectors where female participation is significantly bigger (clothing, beauty, etc.),  and entrepreneurs registered in the "10,000 Women, " entrepreneurship program,  sponsored by the Goldman Sachs Foundation, in partnership with the IDB. The IDB representative in Brazil, Daniela Carrera-Marquis, stresses the importance of such actions, taking into account that Brazil has a growing number of women entrepreneurs. "Brazilian women are spending more time and resources on studying, and now represent 53% of business owners, one percent higher than the average for Latin America and the Caribbean," says Carrera-Marquis. A study by the MIF and FELABAN also show that banks in Latin America and the Caribbean consider SMEs as strategic to their business and are growing and improving policies for financing the sector. In the first stage of the project, Itaú Unibanco will diagnose the needs of women entrepreneurs to draw an overview of operations and financial services that may be more appropriate. The risk assessment matrix to be developed through the project will also incorporate innovative tools and forms of measurement based on factors such as motivation and ethics. Project partners expect, with support from specific products and services, this group of companies run by women will increase revenue and generate more employment than the market average. weB was designed by the beyondBanking, program of the IDB’s Department of Structured and Corporate Finance (SCF), dedicated to the private sector, and by the MIF’s Encouraging Small Business Financing (LAPE) line of activity. In Brazil, the project also has the support of the Getulio Vargas Foundation (FGV) and Fundação Dom Cabral. About Itau Unibanco Itaú Unibanco is one of the largest in the world in market value. With more than 97,000 employees worldwide, has a network of services with nearly 33,000 units in Brazil and abroad. The bank maintains a strong presence in the micro, small and medium enterprises, with a loan portfolio of approximately US$ 45 billion in Brazil.

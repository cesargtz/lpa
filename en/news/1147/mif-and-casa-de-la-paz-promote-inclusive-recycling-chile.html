The MIF and Casa de la Paz promote inclusive recycling in Chile

The MIF and Casa de la Paz promote inclusive recycling in Chile

March 12, 2013
By
Claudia Moreno

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), a member of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB), has approved a $683,000 technical cooperation grant to support a project that will be carried out with the Chilean foundation Casa de la Paz and The National Movement of Recyclers of Chile, to contribute to a more inclusive recycling market in the Metropolitan Region of Santiago. The National Movement of Recyclers of Chile (MNRCh) estimates that there are approximately 60,000 waste pickers (or recyclers) nationwide, who work in vulnerable conditions and earn low incomes. They collect about 70% of the household solid waste that arrives at recycling plants. There are discussions ongoing about public policies that present opportunities for recyclers to be formally included in integral solid waste management systems and recycling value chains, with great potential to generate business partnerships with the private sector. The project aims to incorporate waste pickers and improve their access to markets for recyclable materials (PET, plastic, cardboard, glass, aluminum, paper, etc.) and to solid waste management systems. The project is expected to increase the business volume of collectors’ associations, and thus the average income of their members; and to improve the conditions under which the business of collecting, storing and sale of materials on the market operates. By strengthening the recycling associations’ management and business models and their partnerships with municipalities and the private sector, the project expects to increase both recycling rates and the recyclers’ associations’ sales and revenue. A key player in the project is the “Recycle Santiago Plan,” launched by the Ministry of the Environment, the Metropolitan Municipality and Casa de la Paz Foundation. This program now has the support of more than 40 municipalities in the Metropolitan Region of Chile, and its goal is to recycle 25% of municipal solid waste in the capital in 2020. Recycle Santiago can provide the tools to finance the construction of collection centers and recycling centers, complementing the MIF and Casa de la Paz’s contribution of technical assistance for operating and strengthening the association’s management. In addition, the project has involved private sector partners in the market for consumer goods and recycling, such as The Coca-Cola Company, Gerdau Aza, Sorepa and Tetrapak. The MNRCh has played a key role in the project design representing recyclers. This project in Chile is part of the Regional Initiative for Inclusive Recycling, which aims to contribute to the transformation of the recycling market in Latin America and the Caribbean. In this initiative, the MIF, The Coca-Cola Company, the Avina Foundation and the Water and Sanitation Division of the IDB have joined forces to improve the socioeconomic situation of waste pickers, involving the private sector to facilitate their access to formal markets and improve the regulatory environment for the management of solid waste and recyclable materials markets in Latin America and the Caribbean. About the MIF

The Multilateral Investment Fund (MIF), funded by 39 donors, supports private sector-led development benefitting low-income populations and the poor - their businesses, their farms, and their households. The aim is to give them the tools to boost their incomes: access to markets and the skills to compete in those markets, access to finance, and access to basic services, including green technology. A core MIF mission is to act as a development laboratory - experimenting, pioneering, and taking risks in order to build and support successful micro and SME business models.

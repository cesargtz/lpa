New Directions in Public-Private Partnerships

New Directions in Public-Private Partnerships

March 11, 2013
By
Dennis Blumenfeld

Thursday marks the start of the Annual Meeting of the IDB Board of Governors in Panama. One issue certain to be discussed is the region’s continued infrastructure gap, which hinders productivity and development in many countries due to lack of basic sanitation, safe and efficient modes of transportation, and reliable access to electricity. At the Annual Meeting, there will be a panel organized by the MIF during which top experts will discuss how Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs) can be utilized as an effective contractual approach for infrastructure development and service provision. The panel, New Directions in PPPs: New Actors, New Sectors, and New Sources of Funding, will take place on Friday March 15th from 9:30 a.m. to 10:30 a.m.

Those watching will learn how PPPs can leverage the innovation, expertise and financial capacity of the private sector for financing, designing, building, operating and maintaining infrastructure projects through long-term, output-based contracts. The panel will also explore when and when not to use PPPs and look at new actors, new sectors, and new sources of funding for these projects.  

New Actors

The region’s experience with PPPs has been concentrated in larger and more developed economies. Yet, countries like Jamaica, Trinidad and Tobago, Guatemala, Honduras and Uruguay are developing and formalizing PPP programs to attract private investment for infrastructure development. In the Annual Meeting’s host country, Panama, the cabinet approved a major concession for a container port in Colon, demonstrating the government’s willingness to involve the private sector in infrastructure investment. States and cities are also developing PPP programs. Fiscal decentralization and the responsibility of sub-national governments for projects in certain sectors like water and sanitation and solid waste management have fueled this trend.

New Sectors 

The Minas Gerais PPP unit was responsible for contracting out the first highway PPP in Brazil. Now the state is exploring how PPPs can be utilized for diverse services such as forest conservation. At the Annual Meeting, panel participants will explain advances and challenges for applying PPPs to new and innovative sectors including health, education and beyond. Marcos Siqueira, Executive Manager of the Minas Gerais PPP Unit, and one of the panelists for the session, is well-poised to explain the opportunities and challenges in developing PPPs in smaller and sub-national economies. The MIF is proud to have supported the initial development of the Minas Gerais government PPP unit through one of its projects.    

New Sources of Funding

Traditionally, the largest financiers of PPP projects in Latin America and the Caribbean have been European institutions. Yet, economic problems in Europe—and  worldwide—have led to a retreat of financing from traditional sources. In this context, the importance of regional based sources of funding, such as capital markets, is growing. Panelists Mini Roy, Director and Head of Export and Agency Finance for Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation, and Fabrice Henry, Managing Director at Astris Finance, will discuss conditions for leveraging these sources of funding for PPP projects.

During the session, there will be an overview of the experience with public-private partnerships for infrastructure in Latin America and the Caribbean. The panelists will explain the benefits for governments from partnering with the private sector in terms of financial resources and innovation and key issues in PPPs such as transparency and project bankability. The panelists will also discuss important projects recently developed in Panama and challenges for developing projects in smaller economies and at the sub-national level.  

This panel will build on the success of the recent PPPAmericas conference, which was organized by the MIF and took place last month in Cartagena, Colombia. At PPPAmericas, over 500 participants from Latin America and the Caribbean and throughout the world learned firsthand about the growing importance of new actors, new sectors and new sources of funding for PPPs.   

The panel will be moderated by Patricio Abal, Editor of Latin Infrastructure Quarterly, a specialized publication on infrastructure in LAC. Those not in attendance in Panama will have the opportunity to view the session online in real time through the IDB website.

Clean energy investment and policies: 2014 Climatescope results

Clean energy investment and policies: 2014 Climatescope results

Octubre 28, 2014
By
Michael Hofmann

 
(click to enlarge)

Clean energy has been a key driver of the transformation of the global energy system. Between 2006 and 2013, Latin America and the Caribbean attracted a cumulative US$132bn for renewable energy sources, namely biofuels, biomass, geothermal, solar, small hydro and wind. This significant investment has benefitted countries’ clean energy value chains and has made an important contribution to the region’s low carbon economic development, energy security, and poverty reduction. 

We just released the 2014 Climatescope, a country-by-country assessment, interactive report and index, conceived by the Multilateral Investment Fund in 2012 with a focus on Latin America and the Caribbean. The report sheds light on the increasingly dynamic and complex nature of global clean energy markets. It offers a clear picture of clean energy policy and investment trends in 55 emerging markets in Africa, Asia and Latin America and the Caribbean, now covering over half of the world’s population and approximately a quarter of its GDP.   

The results show that developing nations represent a large and rapidly growing share of the world’s clean energy investment, with demand for clean energy increasing strongly. In fact, clean energy growth rates in the developing world have outpaced those of most developed countries, demonstrated by 142GW of added capacity in Climatescope countries since 2008, representing a 19 percent average annual growth rate, versus 13 percent in OECD countries. 

At a regional level, clean energy provided more than 92TWh in 2013 to the Latin American and Caribbean’s 600m population, representing 6.4% of total energy generation. Adding large hydro generation, which is not included in the Climatescope survey, the region met more than half of its power demand through zero-CO2 emitting power sources.

Clean energy investments: The regional leaders 

With China, Brazil and South Africa leading the 2014 Climatescope index, the Americas region is strongly represented among the ten nations with the highest overall scores: Chile is in 5th place, Uruguay  in 6th, and Mexico in 8th, with Peru following closely at 11th.  At a regional level, these top five countries attracted US$13bn, or an impressive 94% of the US$14bn in total of the 2013 clean energy investment in the 26 countries in the region.

The three nations with the biggest year-on-year improvements are Barbados, Costa Rica and Uruguay. These countries were particularly successful in 2013 regarding effective clean energy policy mechanisms. Uruguay, for example, successfully held reverse auctions for wind and solar projects, resulting in US$1.3bn in clean energy investment in 2013, more than any other nation as a percentage of national GDP. 


Regulatory framework supporting regional clean energy deployment

An important factor that contributed to the Latin American clean energy development has been the increasing deployment of supportive regulation, with 19 countries in the region now having at least one type of supporting policy incentive. In total, the region now has a staggering 150 clean energy policies in place. Tax incentives, auctions, net metering, and biofuel blending mandates constitute the key types of supportive regulation. 

The Climatescope has also been a highly useful tool for regional governments, for example in the context of value chain analysis for national clean energy regulation. This is exactly the intended use that we were aiming for when developing the Climatescope as a tool to overcome information gaps and asymmetries for the various stakeholder along the clean energy value chain. We are very pleased to witness the Climatescope’s continued growth in terms of technical scope and geographical coverage.

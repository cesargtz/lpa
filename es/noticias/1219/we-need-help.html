We need help!

We need help!

Noviembre 14, 2012
By
Monica Pina Alzugaray

Despite the title, this is not the MIF’s latest fundraising tactic or our new recruiting strategy. This is how NXTP Labs’ co-founder Gonzalo Costa ended his presentation at the MIF’s headquarters in Washington, DC yesterday afternoon. The title may very well sum-up the state of our region’s ecosystem for early stage investing: it needs help. The region still falls short on many aspects, starting by a culture that fears and denies failure, an insufficient number of role models, few specialized service providers and seasoned early stage investors. The good news is that investment models that help address some of these challenges, such as the one proposed by NXTP Labs or the Brazilian 21212 accelerator, are appearing in the region and being well received by regional markets.

In front of a mixed crowd at the Inter-American Development Bank, Gonzalo presented NXTP Labs’ accelerator model that funds Internet start-ups in Latin America. So far, NXTP Labs has invested in 55 companies, exited one, and intends to continue taking advantage of the opportunity offered by the region’s growing middle class and rising internet penetration and mobile use, which even if high, still has a long way to go before reaching developed-market levels. Their objective is to complete 300 investments, plus a few follow-ons, in the next nine years.

The model focuses on helping start-ups become investment ready, through a value proposition that consists of four parts. The first intends to overcome, in the words of Gonzalo, “the entrepreneur’s most tangible barrier”: cash. NXTP Labs will provide a $ 25,000 cash investment in start-ups with more than one founder that have shown at least some execution capacity (i.e., they can show something more than just a good idea). The other components are bundled together as part of NXTP Labs’ 13-week accelerating program, which consists of a set of mentoring, trainings and coaching, as well as access to specialized service providers, office space and networking events, where entrepreneurs get to meet potential investors and other relevant figures. In words of NXTP Labs’ co-founder and serial entrepreneur Ariel Arrieta “the accelerating process is how we create value and the investment is how we capture it”. From the MIF’s more than 16 years experience investing in early stage funds, we can only corroborate that access to appropriate mentoring and networks in an entrepreneur’s early stage of development are the most valuable assets and have been key in determining the success of some of our most important success stories.

NXTP Labs emulates successful US-born models such as Y Combinator and Tech Starts, which have incubated companies like DropBox and have seen their companies’ valuation multiples skyrocket. However, in order to reach this point many start-ups have had to fail and business models have had to pivot. Yes, the road to success does make a stop at Failure Town. In yesterday’s presentation, Gonzalo made it clear that even though NXTP Labs does not “celebrate failure” they acknowledge it will happen, and they welcome the learning they will gain from it: “if you are going to fail, you better fail fast”, so you can move on to re-define your business model or think up a new one. Even if the accelerating model implies that a substantial number of start-ups will not survive, it also assumes that entrepreneurs will keep on trying and those who survive will become angel investors, and will support  other entrepreneurs. This results in a continued recycling of early-stage capital that benefits from first-hand experience and demystification of failure.

Another crucial area of the ecosystem where these models have an effect is angel investing. As explained in an earlier post in this blog by the head of MIF’s Early Stage Equity Group, Susana García-Robles, the presence of seasoned angel investors in early-stage companies provides a crucial support for these companies. The way these models can help is by what Gonzalo called “waking-up angels”. This means offering potential angels the opportunity to learn to invest in a new asset class  through a non-direct investment and, for those who are interested, a chance to further their investments, by providing access to a good pipeline. However, the capacity of these models to scale their “evangelization” of angel investors in the region is limited.

The fact that private sector-led models such as the one described, as well as other similar ones, are starting to emerge locally and gain traction, is great news for an institution like the MIF where we have been working to promote the development of a healthy entrepreneurial ecosystem for more than 16 years. Yet, going back to my opening statement, the region is far from having reached that point and continues to face important challenges in areas such as early-stage financing, friendly-legal frameworks, specialized services, presence of role models, among others. 

The good news is that there are an increasing number of both private and public initiatives across the region, focused on helping overcome some of these weaknesses. These initiatives have been welcomed by regional markets and are already achieving good results; a great example is Start-Up Chile. On our side, the MIF continues to follow these challenges and work in partnership with the region’s private and public sectors to HELP develop a healthy ecosystem. So, stay tuned for news on our coming projects and investments!
